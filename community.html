Helpful Links & Support Materials

  • WHO - Dementia Fact Sheet

    Provides global prevalence, projected growth (50-million → 152 million by 2050), and notes dementia-related deaths would rank in the top 3 alongside heart disease and cancer.

  • National Institute on Aging - Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI)

    Defines MCI above normal age-related decline, before severe dementia and stresses early detection can allow reversibility—brain changes often start a decade before symptoms.

  • NINDS - Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)

    Explains how head injuries (concussions, skull fractures, etc.) increase dementia risk up to four-fold.

  • NIH - How Sleep Clears the Brain

    Describes the brain's glymphatic system flushing toxins during sleep, highlighting quality sleep as critical for long-term brain health.

  • Alzheimer's.gov

    The U.S. government's portal for Alzheimer's symptoms, caregiving tips, research updates, and support services.

  • Brain Health Registry

    An online research platform where anyone can register, track their memory over time, and contribute data to accelerate dementia research.

  • Hawaii Fluid Art

    Local art-making experiences for older adults—“Looking at or making art can help patients with Alzheimer's feel calmer and more engaged.”

  • Expressive Arts Hawai'i

    O'ahu-based expressive arts therapies that promote emotional, social, cognitive, and physical integration through art.

  • Music & Memory

    A program that brings personalized music into care settings—shown to animate and engage dementia patients through familiar songs.
